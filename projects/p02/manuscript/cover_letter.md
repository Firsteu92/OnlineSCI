Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "**Conditional robustness of equatorial Kelvin waves in the real ocean from multi-mission altimetry and SWOT snapshots**," for consideration as an Article in *Nature Communications*.

In 2017, Delplace, Marston and Venaille showed in *Science* that equatorial Kelvin waves are topologically protected edge modes of the rotating shallow-water system — a prediction with profound implications for how the ocean's most consequential waves (the carriers of El Niño signals) survive their journey across a turbulent basin. Whether this protection operates in the real ocean has never been tested observationally. Our manuscript provides that first test, and the answer reshapes the question itself.

Three results merit the attention of a broad readership:

1. **The first observational test of topological wave protection in the ocean.** Combining multi-mission altimetry event tracking (7 independent Kelvin wave events during the 2023–2024 El Niño, verified against ERA5 westerly wind burst forcing) with SWOT wide-swath snapshots and three independent control groups, we find perturbation-type-dependent robustness: amplitude preservation at island chains, loss in tropical instability wave (TIW) zones.

2. **Perturbation amplitude is not what breaks the protection — resonance is.** The natural gap-to-perturbation ratio Λ fails to distinguish perturbation zones in real data at any spatial scale: island wakes and TIW vortex cores carry the same local vorticity yet produce opposite wave outcomes. We show why: the spectral gap forbids elastic scattering, and only perturbations carrying energy at the wavenumber–frequency offset connecting the Kelvin mode to the westward wave band can break the protection. TIWs sit precisely at that resonant window; island wakes are wavenumber-mismatched by an order of magnitude. A resonance-window criterion Λ₂ built on this physics tracks per-event amplitude loss within the TIW zone (r = 0.69, n = 7), with the expected absence of correlation at focusing-dominated island chains.

3. **A pre-registered path to definitive attribution.** All diagnostics — event detection, deduplication, multi-control bootstrap statistics, and the Λ₂ criterion — are released as reproducible code, and the Λ₂–amplitude correlation constitutes a falsifiable hypothesis now being tested against the full 1993–2025 altimetric record.

We believe this work will interest the topological-physics community (first oceanic field test of a landmark theory), the climate community (Kelvin waves are the conveyor of ENSO onset), and the satellite-oceanography community (a concrete SWOT-era observational framework). We have been deliberate in reporting both positive and null results with full statistical honesty.

This manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. All data sources are public (CMEMS, AVISO/SWOT, ERA5, Argo); analysis code will be deposited upon acceptance. We declare no competing interests.
