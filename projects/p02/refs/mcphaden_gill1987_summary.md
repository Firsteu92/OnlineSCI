# McPhaden & Gill (1987) — L2 Summary by ClaudeB

> Prepared for P02 review context. This paper is the key "classical scattering" baseline against which P02's topological framework must demonstrate added value.

**Citation**: McPhaden, M. J., & Gill, A. E. (1987). Topographic scattering of equatorial Kelvin waves. *J. Phys. Oceanogr.*, 17, 82–96.

## Model

Linear, reduced-gravity, two active layers above a deep resting layer. Equatorial long-wave approximation (nondispersive Kelvin + Rossby only). Topography: infinitely long meridional submarine ridge, infinitesimally thin, completely blocking flow in the lower active layer.

## Key Predictions

1. Incident first-baroclinic-mode Kelvin wave → scattered into:
   - Transmitted first-mode Kelvin (reduced amplitude)
   - Reflected first-mode Rossby waves (backscattering)
   - Transmitted + reflected second-mode Kelvin and Rossby waves (inter-modal transfer)

2. Scattering controlled by two dimensionless parameters:
   - **r = H₀/H₁** (upper/lower active layer thickness ratio)
   - **γ = (ρ₁ - ρ₀)/(ρ₂ - ρ₁)** (density jump ratio)

3. Scattering **strong** when r ~ O(1), γ ~ O(1) (comparable layers)
4. Scattering **weak** when r >> 1 or r << 1 (one layer dominates)

## Relation to Delplace et al. (2017)

No contradiction. They answer different questions:

| Aspect | McPhaden & Gill | Delplace et al. |
|---|---|---|
| Question | How much energy scatters? | Why do Kelvin/Yanai modes exist? |
| Topography | Explicit ridge | None (flat bottom) |
| Scattering | Quantified (Kelvin → Rossby + mode-2) | Not addressed |
| Protection claim | N/A | Mode existence guaranteed by Chern number |

Topology guarantees the Kelvin mode persists in the dispersion relation; it does not prevent energy redistribution when topography breaks translational symmetry.

## Relevance to P02's Λ Parameter

P02's Λ = Δω_eff / Δω_perturbation quantifies the crossover:
- Large Λ → topological existence dominates, scattering weak
- Small Λ → classical scattering physics (McPhaden & Gill) dominates

McPhaden & Gill's r and γ parameters relate to P02's framework: they control how effectively topography couples baroclinic modes, which maps to the "perturbation strength" in the Λ denominator.

## Majda et al. (1999) — Related

Topographic resonance can transfer energy from nonlinear Kelvin waves to Yanai/Rossby modes at specific zonal wavenumbers set by topographic wavenumber. Nonlinear mechanism — disperses Kelvin wave and slows its propagation. Relevant to P02's island chain scattering scenarios.
