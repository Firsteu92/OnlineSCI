# Calval Period Kelvin Wave Availability (ClaudeB Research)

> Result: YES — at least 2 Kelvin wave events during SWOT calval fast-sampling (March 26 – July 10, 2023).

## Key Finding

The SWOT calval period coincided with the first Kelvin wave pulses of the 2023-24 El Nino. This is highly favorable for P02.

## Timeline

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| Jan 2023 | La Nina weakening; small Kelvin wave near Micronesia, dissipated by mid-Feb |
| Feb-Mar 2023 | WWBs in western-central Pacific (MJO-driven) |
| Early Mar 2023 | **First significant downwelling Kelvin wave emerges, propagates eastward** |
| Mar 26, 2023 | SWOT calval fast-sampling orbit begins (~1-day repeat) |
| Mar-Apr 2023 | Sentinel-6 detects Kelvin waves: 5-10 cm SSH anomalies, hundreds of km wide |
| Apr 13, 2023 | NOAA CPC issues El Nino Watch |
| Late Apr 2023 | Kelvin wave reaches eastern Pacific (Peru/Ecuador coast) |
| May 2023 | "One Kelvin wave passed through, another emerging" (NOAA). 4th warmest May subsurface on record. |
| Jun 2023 | NOAA declares El Nino conditions developed |
| Jul 10, 2023 | SWOT calval orbit ends |

## Implications for P02

1. **At least 2 Kelvin wave events** within calval window — enough for 2D structural analysis with ~1-day repeat
2. **5-10 cm SSH anomalies** — well above SWOT KaRIn noise floor at mesoscale wavelengths
3. **TIWs intensifying** during El Nino onset in eastern Pacific — natural scattering obstacles for SQ2
4. **Q03 from R01 answered**: calval period has usable events. Phase 1 should prioritize calval data.

## Caveats

- Need to verify SWOT calval orbit ground track coverage at specific equatorial Pacific longitudes
- Earliest calval data (late March) may have higher noise (commissioning phase)
- Kelvin wave SSH signal is large-scale; SWOT's unique value is resolving *meridional structure*
