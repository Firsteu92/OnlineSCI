# P05 Editorial Decision — R0

**Journal:** Geophysical Research Letters
**Manuscript:** SWOT KaRIn Constraints on the Deep Ocean Mixing Energy Deficit
**Decision: Major Revision — scope must be substantially revised**
**Date:** 2026-06-08

---

Dear Authors,

The deep ocean mixing energy deficit (Munk & Wunsch 1998) remains one of the great unsolved problems in physical oceanography. A credible new observational constraint would be of exceptional interest. However, the current manuscript's scope significantly exceeds what the data and methodology can support.

## Revision Requirements

### R1. Replace synthetic data with real observations

All analysis is based on synthetic data generated by random number generators. The claimed result ("missing energy reduced from ~1 TW to 0.4–0.7 TW") is a hardcoded number in the plotting script, not a computed result. This must be completely rebuilt using real SWOT and ancillary data.

### R2. The surface-to-deep inference chain is physically broken

The manuscript claims: SSH -> wavenumber spectrum -> internal tide energy flux -> mixing efficiency -> energy budget closure. Each link has critical issues:

**(a) SSH anomaly -> internal tide energy flux:**
Separating internal tide SSH signals from mesoscale eddies, wind-driven response, and steric effects in a single SWOT snapshot requires sophisticated space-time filtering. The M2 internal tide is temporally coherent (predictable from barotropic forcing), but the incoherent component (modulated by mesoscale eddies) is not separable from a single satellite pass. Zhao et al. (2016) used 20+ years of along-track altimetry to isolate the coherent M2 internal tide — SWOT's ~2-year record with 21-day repeat cannot replicate this approach.

**(b) Surface energy flux -> deep mixing:**
The vertical energy pathway involves: internal tide generation at topography -> vertical propagation through the thermocline -> reflection, scattering, cascade to smaller scales -> turbulent dissipation -> diapycnal mixing. Each step has its own efficiency factor. Garrett & Kunze (2007) parameterize the internal wave-internal tide interaction step, not the entire surface-to-deep pathway. Applying their parameterization to SSH-derived energy flux requires vertical stratification (N-squared), internal wave field characteristics, and local topographic roughness — none of which come from SWOT SSH alone.

**(c) Regional vs. global:**
Closing the global 2 TW budget requires global coverage. A regional energy flux estimate is valuable but cannot be extrapolated to a global budget without strong assumptions.

### R3. Reduce the scope of claims

The claimed 30–50% reduction in missing energy is extraordinary. The community has spent 25 years working on this problem (Vic et al. 2019, Waterhouse et al. 2014). Claims must be proportional to the evidence.

**Recommended scope — choose one:**

**Option A (GRL-appropriate):** "SWOT reveals enhanced submesoscale SSH variance in the Luzon Strait internal tide generation region: implications for energy flux estimates"
- One well-studied region (Luzon Strait or Hawaiian Ridge)
- SWOT vs. conventional altimetry wavenumber spectrum comparison
- Quantify additional SSH variance in the 15–50 km band
- Discuss implications for energy flux without claiming to close the global budget

**Option B (JGR-Oceans, more ambitious):** "Constraining internal tide energy flux in the South China Sea using SWOT SSH, Argo stratification, and microstructure observations"
- Combine SWOT (surface), Argo (vertical structure), and microstructure database (ground truth)
- Build the energy pathway for one region with three independent constraints
- Report the regional residual
